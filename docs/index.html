A wallpaper picker. Nothing more.

Most wallpaper apps want to be a service. They sync. They make accounts. They recommend. Mural reads a folder.

A small app. Lives in your menu bar. Opens when you press the hotkey. Changes the wallpaper when you click one. No cloud. No account. Nothing to subscribe to.

You bring the pictures. Drop them in ~/Mural, or hand Mural a link to a public gallery on GitHub. Mural notices new files right away and pulls in fresh ones from the gallery on a schedule you pick.

❦

Two ways to feed it.

A folder on your Mac, or any public gallery on GitHub. Drop a picture in your folder and it appears in the picker right away. Point Mural at a gallery and it pulls new wallpapers down in the background — on whatever schedule you pick.

Galleries on GitHub are free, and there are plenty of them already. Mural ships with a few good ones built in, so you have somewhere to start before you bring your own.

❦

How it works.

Drop wallpapers in ~/Mural. Press ⌘⇧W from anywhere on your Mac. Click a thumbnail. The wallpaper changes the same instant. Hit Esc and the picker goes away. You go back to whatever you were doing.

❦

Honest beta notes.

It's a 0.1. Some edges are still rough.

  • Not yet signed by Apple. The first time you open it, macOS will warn you. Right-click the app and choose Open — you only do this once.
  • Built for Intel Macs first. Runs on Apple Silicon through Rosetta. A native build is next.
  • Lock-screen wallpapers work, but not as cleanly as the desktop. macOS keeps that one a bit guarded.
  • Video wallpapers show up as a still frame for now. Animated playback is coming.
  • A different wallpaper per Space, and auto-rotate at sunrise & sunset, are wired up but not finished.
  • iPhone HEIC photos work. They lean on macOS to read them, so the very first open is a touch slower.
